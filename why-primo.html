why primo

Making Websites is a PITA

Despite all the advancements that have been made in web development and content management, developers looking to build small, custom, easily-editable sites still find themselves wrangling a hodgepodge of finicky build scripts, complex frameworks, and demanding CMSs - things that work well enough for big sites, but are overkill for smaller projects. Primo solves that by putting all those pieces together into a single simple, powerful application, and then some.

Picture this: your close friend starts a coffee shop and asks you to make them a website. You’re excited. You start thinking about the possibilities - the color palette, the photography, the messaging. You start looking at other sites and finding inspiration. Your excitement builds. This is gonna to be your Sistine Chapel, your Mona Lisa. But more than that, it's gonna be fun.

But first, the hard part. This site needs to be editable so you'll have to find a simple, easy, ideally free CMS. And a framework to turn the content from it into HTML. And a local environment so you can see what your code is actually going to look like. Not to mention setting up Tailwind (because if you're going this far you might as well put in a little extra work to speed up development).

You struggle your way through obscure build errors and outdated documentation. You plugin some plugins and when they don't work you plug in some other plugins. You post a question in Stack Overflow and it gets closed for being a duplicate. At some point your friend asks you how the site's going and if they could take a look at your progress so far. You stall and say you haven't had a chance to get started yet.

For someone who's primary job description is writing HTML & CSS, you sure have spent a lot of time not writing HTML & CSS. But that’s okay, because after what feels like forever you manage to put all the pieces, build the site, and begrudgingly apply security updates every month until your friend decides to hire a marketing firm down the road to build their new site and you happily hand it over.
